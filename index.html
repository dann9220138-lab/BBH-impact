Bali Blue Harmony — Impact Visuals

Coral

The coral data consists of two parts: coral cover on reef star compared to coral cover in nature and coral diversity in 2026 monitoring.

The first part adopts two chart types: line chart and range plot. The tradeoff in the line chart is that audience is more familiar with this type where they can understand the chart fast, but this chart could be "boring" sometimes. In contrast, rangle plot is less common and effectively highlights the closing gap of coral cover on reef star and in nature.



The second part visualizes the coral diversity in the newest survey, where curious audience can further explore our data. Future coral diversity data can be simply updated to the current design so that we can compare year by year.

Fish

The fish diversity data has three parts: important diversity metrics, fish biomass, & fish diversity detail.

The grouped columns condense three important metrics: number of families and species, & abundance.


The area chart further highlights how our reef stars inhabit and support more reef fish through the lense of biomass.


The third part adopts two visual types: family tree and searchable table. Still some tradeoffs between the two charts: family tree is more visually appealing and fun to look at and supports a quick year-by-year comparison using the time button on top, but requires more mental processing power. However, the seachable table is more straightforward and allows users to search specific fish or new species, but it lacks general magnitude. If facing public, the family tree is recommended, the seachable table can be internally used.


Community Empowerment

The community data is visualized through diagram/poster style. Also can add some future goals.


For all the above visuals, they are easily updated with new data, embedded in any forms, and adjusted its design (color, font, size, title...).
